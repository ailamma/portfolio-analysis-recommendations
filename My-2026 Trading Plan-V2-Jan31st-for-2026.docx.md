**TRADING PLAN OBJECTIVE:**  
The objective of the trading plan is to define my goals for this account, portfolio management guidelines, trading rules, risk management, position sizing  and trade strategies. It's all about probabilities and strategies. Consistently place trades to let the probabilities work out.  **Be consistent, Mechanical and Repeatable\!**

**GOALS FOR THE ACCOUNT:**

* The goal in Trading Account is to generate 2-3% income monthly (25-30% annual) on the Net Liquidation (Net Liq) value to grow the account to 1 million by 2029\.  
* The focus is on generating monthly income for growth of the account.  
* The goal is to construct a portfolio so that I do not have to become consumed with watching the market daily and hourly.


**PORTFOLIO GREEKS:**  
The goal of the portfolio is to be as close to Delta neutral as possible depending on the current market environment and to have as much Theta as we can to drive the income generation of the account WITHOUT exceeding our BP limitations.  We monitor these 2 main Greeks as follows:

1. **Delta**: Not more than 0.2% of Net Liquidation value BETA weighted to SPY  
2. **Theta**: 0.3% of Net Liq is the targeted minimum.  
3. **Vega**: 1-1.5X Theta or less is desirable to reduce volatility exposure.

**RISK MANAGEMENT:**

* Buying power is a measure of portfolio risk and is the main driver of portfolio management and the main indicator for monitoring account health.  
* The goal is to be at or below 50% of available Buying Power in normal environments.  
  * 40-50% is the goal with futures and accounting for Span Margin.  
    * If BP is over 60%, no NEW trades should be initiated until trades are closed or BP is back under 60%.  
    * **In higher volatility times, you can exceed the 50% BP rule, but stay on alert for margin increases and watch positions**.  
      * Mine: when VIX spikes double digits, start adding RMCCs , **within max BP of 60%**  
    * Never have more than 85% BP at any given time. Take action to reduce BP in that case.  
      * **To reduce BP if in a state of alert/concern:**  
        * **1\. Close a winning trade,**   
        * **2\. Close short puts of a 112 PDS,**  
        * **3\. Close smaller losers,**  
        * **4\. Close BIL position,**  
        * **5\. Add a hedge using long put**  
* **Never risk more than 2% of my total portfolio on any one single trade**.  
* Never invest more than 40% of my max allowed BP on any one strategy

**TRADE RULES:**

1. **Plan your trade and then trade your plan. NEVER trade without a plan for exit and entry\!**  
2. NEVER modify or break the trade rules during a trade.   
3. Stay mechanical and trade without emotion.  
4. NEVER chase the entry, there will be another trade.  
5. If I have to ask whether I should be in the trade, then get out of that trade.  
6. NEVER revenge trade or try to show the market that it is wrong\!  Accept the fact that the market is always right.  
7. NEVER average down or add to a losing position.    
8. Nothing wrong with closing a winner, getting back in is just another trade away.  
9. Holding on to a position overnight is the same thing as buying that stock or option at the end of the day.  
10. Never trade looking at profits first \- Look at max loss and size accordingly.  
11. NEVER trade just because you are bored or are looking just to trade.  
12. Track EVERY trade by strategy and analyze on a weekly & monthly basis.   
13. Hope is not a trading strategy, if you find yourself hoping a trade moves a certain way, exit the trade.  
14. Let the probabilities work by consistently placing trades over time.  
15. Hedge ONLY what needs to be hedged.  
16. Keep it simple.  “Simplify to Multiply\!”  
17. NEVER try to time the market.  Don’t leg into trades trying to get a better deal on each leg.  The market is always smarter.

**CORE STRATEGIES:**

**CALENDARIZED 11x TRADE (/ES 112c)**

This trade has a much shorter DTE for the naked puts, thus, they have a tad more risk, but come off quicker and we are left with a PDS for nearly 2-3 months.  The ladder of PDS will help with BP relief and Delta hedging.

**BUYING POWER & SIZING:**

1. Trades using /ES  
2. Size for max loss per trade of 2% of Net Liq portfolio value. Increase size as needed within the BP limit.

**TRADE ENTRY:**

1. Enter a 100 wide PDS at 150DTE (120DTE is also an option) @ 15-20 delta long put for around $8-10db (Can change based on market profile)  
2. Finance with 2 naked puts at 60DTE around 7-9 delta to generate the desired credit. (I am aiming for $15-16cr on the NPs)

**MANAGEMENT:**  
**NOTE: I manage the Naked Puts (NP) and PDS as 2 separate trades.**

**Naked Put Management Options:**

1. When NP are at 90% of the initial credit on the trade, close them, this frees up BP and makes the trade a FREE PDS hedge for 90+ days.  
2. Close them at a 2X loss.  
3. Roll them out (and down) if touched or near expiration  
   1. NOTE: You can close/adjust the PDS to pay for the roll  
4. Take Assignment if BP allows (Then Wheel out with ATM Covered Calls)

**Put Debit Spread (PDS) Management:**	  
NOTE: If any leg of the spread that is in the money (ITM) at expiration must be closed to avoid being exercised or assigned and fees. 

1. **REDUCE RISK:**  
   1. If the short put of the PDS hits 90-95%+ profitability, you can close that contract and leave the long put open as a full hedge.  
      1. NOTE: This will reduce income generation slightly.  
2. **INCREASE GAINS:**  
   1. Wait until close to expiration to close at or near full spread profit.  
   2. If PDS is profitable early, we can look to capture part of the TRAP max profit by rolling the short put up by 50% or more of the width of the PDS to lock in profit and still have a TRAP remaining.  
      1. NOTE: Rolling up the short put of the PDS will keep the TRAP size larger and at a higher strike, thus creating a greater chance for the remaining adjusted PDS to pay off.

**Options:**  
**Income Generators:** Roll up the naked puts, Roll up the short puts of the PDS, Close some of the long puts, Roll the whole PDS down.  
**Hedge Generators:** Remove the naked puts early, Remove some of the naked puts, Remove the shorts of the PDS, Remove just a few of the shorts of the PDS, Close some naked puts and use the cash to roll up the long puts (make a wider trap)

**Income PMCC (Poor Mans Covered Call) Trade**  
We are looking strictly for income and NOT capital appreciation in this version of the PMCC. The goal is making the Extrinsic value of the covered calls over time.

**TRADE ENTRY:**

1. Technicals  
   1. Up Trending Weekly Chart   
      1. 8EMA \> 21EMA (Weekly)  
   2. Ideal RSI under 50,   
   3. Reversing/Bouncing off support/Oversold level,  
   4. Ideally on a pullback to the 50EMA/100MA/200MA  
2. High Quality/Stable Growth Stocks/Assets  
   1. Revenue & Earnings Growth \> 20%  
   2. ROE \> 15%  
   3. Strong market position, exciting products  
3. Enter trade @ 180-365+ DTE (Depends on your time horizon and setup)  
   1. Can go 12-24+ Months if underlying is at lower end of ranges  
4. Long Position  
   1. Buy Long Call (.70-.90 Delta range \- @ .80 Delta ideal)  
   2. Buy Synthetic Stock (ATM long cal \+ short put) to give higher Delta & lower the cost (increasing ROi)  
5. Sell the Calls around 7DTE (Can go 30DTE)  
   1. NOTE: Aim for .75-1% extrinsic to stock price  
   2. 8\>21EMA \- Sell ATM  
   1. 8\<21EMA \- Sell ITM to reduce risk (Use 9 day ATR or the expected move)

**MANAGEMENT:**

1. Covered Calls  
   1. Close the short call when 80-90% of the EXTRINSIC is reached or if not threatened, let it expire  
      1. NOTE: IF ITM at expiration MUST close to avoid assignment of short shares.  
      2. NOTE: Being ITM near expiration with low extrinsic value runs the risk of early assignment.  
   2. Can close the covered call early for more than 50% profit in \<= 50% of the time.   
   3. Can close early and roll if 75% of extrinsic is captured with time remaining.  
   4. NOTE: If a large move is made (Deep ITM/OTM call) in the stock and a roll is needed, may consider only rolling 50-75% of the distance to avoid a “whipsaw” move  
2. Long Calls/Synthetic Longs  
   1. Consider closing the long trade if the loss on the long calls minus gains on the CCs is more than 30%.  
   2. 50%+ gains on the entire trade. (CCs \+ appreciation of longs) \- Depending on time frame (30% in a few months (3-6) could also trigger)  
   3. If the LEAPS is approaching expiration (e.g., within 2-3 months), I would close the entire trade to avoid time decay (theta) eroding the LEAPS' value rapidly.  
3. Closed the entire trade if the Short Call is so Deep In-the-Money that it makes no sense to roll  
4. Look to close the entire trade If the outlook on the underlying stock or market changes (e.g., due to fundamental shifts, economic conditions, or technical indicators).

NOTE: Going ITM on the CC’s is OK.  We are looking for the decay of the Extrinsic premium for income.  If ITM, the losses due to the gain of intrinsic value on the CC’s will be offset by the gains on the long calls.  We want the net gain/loss of the long calls \+ the net gain/loss of the CC’s to be positive and generate strong ROI.
